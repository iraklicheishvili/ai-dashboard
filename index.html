Paper of the week 8.2 / 10
Gated DeltaNet-2: Decoupling Erase and Write in Linear Attention
Hatamizadeh · Hatamizadeh et al. · 3 authors · arXiv:2605.22791v1 · May 21, 2026
AI
Plain-english summary
This paper introduces Gated DeltaNet-2, a more efficient alternative to standard attention that uses a fixed-size memory rather than growing one. The key innovation is splitting a single control knob into two separate ones—one deciding how much old information to erase and another deciding how much new information to write—giving the model finer control over its compressed memory. Tested at 1.3B parameters, it outperforms competing efficient architectures like Mamba-2, Mamba-3, and Kimi Delta Attention, especially on long-context retrieval tasks.
Why it matters: Long-context efficiency is one of the biggest bottlenecks in deploying large language models, since standard attention costs grow quadratically with sequence length and consume enormous memory at inference. Gated DeltaNet-2 offers a path to constant-memory decoding while actually improving accuracy over leading linear-attention rivals, which could lower the cost of serving models that handle long documents, codebases, or agent histories. If the gains hold at larger scales, this kind of architectural refinement could become a standard ingredient in next-generation efficient foundation models.
